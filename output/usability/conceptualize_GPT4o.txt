### Conceptualization of "Personal Innovativeness in IT"

**Definition:**
Personal Innovativeness in IT (PIIT) refers to an individual's willingness to try out new information technologies. It reflects the degree to which a person is open to and interested in adopting new IT solutions.

**Property Type:**
Latent construct

**Applicable Entity:**
Individuals, particularly those who interact with or are potential users of information technology.

**Characteristics:**
- **Openness to New Experiences:** A tendency to be open-minded and curious about new IT developments.
- **Willingness to Experiment:** A readiness to try out new IT tools and applications.
- **Proactive Behavior:** Actively seeking out new IT solutions and staying updated with technological advancements.
- **Risk-Taking:** Comfort with the uncertainties and potential risks associated with adopting new IT.

**Dimensionality:**
Unidimensional – The construct is typically measured as a single dimension reflecting the overall level of innovativeness in IT.

**Context:**
- **Workplace:** Adoption of new software, tools, and systems.
- **Education:** Use of new educational technologies and platforms.
- **Personal Use:** Engagement with new apps, gadgets, and online services.

**Related Constructs:**
- **Technology Acceptance:** The degree to which an individual is willing to accept and use new technology.
- **Innovativeness:** General tendency to be innovative, not limited to IT.
- **Risk Aversion:** The degree to which an individual is averse to taking risks, which can inversely affect PIIT.
