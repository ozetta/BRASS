Construct: Perceived Ease of Use (PEOU)

Definition: The degree to which a person believes that using a particular system would be free of effort.

Property Type: Psychological construct

Applicable Entity: Individuals interacting with technology

Characteristics: PEOU is a subjective construct that can vary between individuals and technologies. It is influenced by factors such as user experience, familiarity with technology, and the complexity of the system.

Dimensionality: Unidimensional

Context: PEOU is particularly relevant in the context of human-computer interaction, where it is used to evaluate the usability of various technologies, such as software applications, websites, and devices.

Related Constructs: Perceived Usefulness, Attitude Towards Using Technology, Self-Efficacy, Computer Anxiety, User Satisfaction, Usability, and User Experience.

PEOU is closely related to Perceived Usefulness (PU), as users are more likely to find a system useful if they perceive it as easy to use. A positive attitude towards using technology, high self-efficacy, and low computer anxiety are also associated with higher PEOU. User satisfaction, usability, and user experience are outcomes that can be influenced by PEOU.
